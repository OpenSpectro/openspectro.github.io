OpenSpectro

3D Spectroscopic Profiling for Biomedical Analysis

Abstract

Spectroscopic analysis plays a crucial role in identifying absorbance-based molecular signaturesâ€”distinct optical patterns observed across various wavelengths. Recognizing these signatures provides valuable insights for designing wearable health-monitoring devices. Specifically, constructing three-dimensional (3D) spectroscopic graphs of molecular spectra enables the optimization of multi-photoplethysmography (PPG) sensors, enhancing their accuracy and performance. Historically, the limitations of computational power and the prevalence of single-wavelength approaches have hindered comprehensive analysis of 3D molecular spectral data, slowing progress in multiwavelength sensor development. However, multi-PPG readings on wearable devices capture more detailed information and effectively track biomarkers, underscoring the need for 3D spectroscopic analysis in studying substances. To bridge this gap, we introduce OpenSpectro, an open-source 3D spectroscopic profiling platform designed for visualizing and sharing molecular spectrum data, particularly those relevant to physiological biomarkers. Its interactive visualization tools empower researchers to compare, explore, and collaborate on spectral patterns, fostering innovations in wearable technology and enabling more precise physiological monitoring.
